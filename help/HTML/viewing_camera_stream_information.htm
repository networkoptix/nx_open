Items on Layouts and Scene

Video Item represents a video from camera of local file played back within DW Spectrum.

Items displayed on Scene provide a lot features and useful information about streams being played.

Local videos are displayed as following:

At the upper left corner of a local video item the file name is displayed.

At the upper right corner of a local video item the following buttons are displayed:

• – Rotation (see "Rotate")

• – Information: displays additional information about the stream being played (see below)

• – Close (removes the item from the current layout).

Streams from cameras are displayed as following:

At the upper left corner of a camera item the following information is displayed:

•Camera Name

•Current recording mode (as camera may be recorded differently depending on schedule):

• – Recording always (green circle)

• – Motion Recording (red circle)

• – Low Quality recording, High quality on motion (red circle crossed green)

• – Not recorded (grey circle).

For more information about recording settings see "Setting up Recording Schedule".

At the upper right corner of a camera item the following buttons are displayed in addition to those displayed for the local video:

• – PTZ (if camera supports) – see "Setting up PTZ"

• – Smart Search – see "Performing Smart Motion Search".

Additional information can be useful during camera setup to help adjust the desired FPS and bitrate.  It may be useful for local files  monitoring as well.

To view item information, click on in the top right corner of the item. To view information for several items select them (see "Selecting items"), open Context Menu and choose Show Info (Alt+i).

The following data will appear on the bottom:

•Current FPS of the stream

•Current bitrate

•Current stream codec (H264 or MJPEG)

•Current quality (if dual-streaming is enabled for the camera)

•Current time of video playback or LIVE if viewing live from a camera.

Additionally, the following messages can be displayed for camera items:

•NO SIGNAL – camera is offline

•NO DATA – if seeking to the archive place record hasn't been performed at

•Loading... – waiting data from Media Server

•Unauthorized – login and/or password for the camera has not been entered or incorrect.
